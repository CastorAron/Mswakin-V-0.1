Destination
Serengeti National Park –
Serengeti National Park is undoubtedly the best known wildlife sanctuary in the world, unequalled for its natural beauty and scientific value, it has the greatest concentration of plains game in Africa. Millions of wildebeest pass every year during their famous migration following the rain to reach green pastures, crossing the Mara River bordering Kenya, diving in waters infested by crocodiles with large herds of zebras and gazelles following them in their quest for new pastures.
Through the end of the migration between August and October, the park still houses a few herds of resident wildebeest, also large number of buffaloes, elephants, giraffes and impalas. Some beautiful species of birds like ostriches, eagles, secretary birds can be found in the park. The park harbors over 500 bird species across the endless plains with Ndutu area taking half of the bird species population. Serengeti is also a home of large population of predators: lions, leopards, cheetahs, hyenas, jackals and servals. The landscape of Serengeti are characterized by its vast plains, large termite mounds, rivers and its “kopjes” (large round rocks on which the lions like to rest)
Located: (Mara - Tanzania), Serengeti lies 350 km from the city of Arusha and next to the Maasai Mara park at the border of Kenya
Activities: Game drive, birding, hot air baloon
Ngorongoro Conservation Area
The Ngorongoro Conservation Area, a UNESCO protected World Heritage Site is situated between Lake Manyara and Serengeti National Park and near by the Eyasi and Natron lakes. It is a leading experiment in multi-purpose land use where people, their livestock and wildlife coexist and share the same protected habitat harmoniously. The most inhabitants in the Ngorongoro Conservation Area are Maasai shepherds who are allowed to live there in freedom. The famous wildebeest migration crosses the Ngorongoro Conservation Area between December and March. Other migrators like zebras and impalas follow this migration and also predators, attracted by this high concentration of ungulates
The Ngorongoro Crater lies in the Crater Highlands of North Tanzania and is within the Ngorongoro Conservation Area. A tour of Ngorongoro Crater begins at the crater rim, with spectacular views from above and continues with a game drive within the Crater itself. The crater is one of the few amazingly beautiful places where the famous Big Five can be observed.
Visitors experience acacia woodlands and lakes teeming with birds, plains filled with zebras, antelopes and elands. Ancient hippos and endangered black rhino have their homes in Ngorongoro Crater. The crater floor consists of a number of different habitats that include grassland, swamps, forests, tens of thousands of plain animals like wildebeest, gazelles, and large predator population of lions, hyena, jackals. Lake Makat (Maasai for ‘salt’) a central soda lake filled by Munge River.
Ndutu is located in the Ngorongoro Conservation Area in the southeastern plains of the Serengeti ecosystem. It is a meeting point between these two incredible wilderness areas. This section of the park is made up of rolling grasslands peppered with alkaline lakes that attract flocks of flamingos as well as acacia woodlands. The best months to visit Ndutu Region are from December when the great migration herds start to arrive from the north, until April when the millions of wildebeest, zebra, and gazelle have trekked northwards again. In the early months of the year – with a peak in February – the wildebeest herds are calving on the verdant grassy plains; thousands upon thousands of baby animals are born every day, attracting the attention of cheetahs, lions and hyenas
Located: (Arusha – Tanzania), 190 km west of Arusha. About 2:30h from Arusha, near to the small town of Karatu.
Activities: Game drive, walking safaris on the edge of the crater with a ranger of the conservation area, visit manyattas (Maasaai villages), trek to the Olmoti and Empakaai craters
Tarangire National Park
The Tarangire National Park is most popular for its large elephant herds and mini-wildlife migration that takes place during the dry season which sees about 250,000 animals enter the park. Tarangire’s sparse vegetation, strewn with baobab and acacia trees, makes it beautiful and distinctive location to visit. It is a good choice for travellers who wish to encounter more animals and less tourists while on safari.
Within the northern part of Tarangire is the permanent River Tarangire also known as the life-line of the park particularly in the dry season when most of the region is totally dry. This flows northwards until it exits the park in the northwestern corner to pour into Lake Burungi.
Herds up to 300 elephants scratch the dry river bed for underground streams, while migratory wildebeest, zebra, buffalo, impala, gazelle, hartebeest and eland crowd the shrinking lagoons. It is the greatest concentration of wildlife outside the Serengeti ecosystem-a smorgasbord of predators and the one place in Tanzania where dry country antelope such as the stately fringe-eared oryx and peculiar long necked gerenuk are regularly observed
Tarangire National Park is known as a wonderful birding destination with over 500 species of birds, including some species endemic to Tanzania. On drier ground you find the Kori bustard, the heaviest flying bird; the stocking - thighed ostrich, the world’s largest bird; and small parties of ground hornbills blustering like turkeys
Located: (Manyara – Tanzania), 118 kms (75 miles) southwest of Arusha
Activities – Day trips to Maasai and Barbaig villages as well as to the hundreds of ancient rock painting in the vicinity of Kolo on the Dodoma Road
Lake Manyara National Park
Located at the foot of Rift Valley escarpment in the Northern part of Tanzania, Lake Manyara National Park is of great beauty and offers amazing wildlife viewing experience. The lake, which represents two thirds of the park, is home to s bird fauna of high quality. Many species of birds live near the lake, including thousands of flamingos which give a pinkish color to its waters. Tree climbing lions also can be seen during safaris in the park and they are among the unique attractions. Lake Manyara National Park is known for having large herds of elephants which can be seen as tourists explore the destinations. Other varieties of wildlife species that can be seen in Lake Manyara National Park include klipspringers, warthogs, impalas, buffalos, hippos, gazelles, lions, leopards among others. The tree species which can be seen in Lake Manyara National Park include acacia trees, baobab trees, doum trees as well as umbrella trees
Lake Manyara National Park is considered as an all year round game park however the dry season from July to November is considered the best time for game viewing. Tourists can also explore the wilderness of Lake Manyara National Park on foot as they engage in nature walking excursion including treetop walkway as well as the majimoto – hot spring trail at the destination. The activity gives a close encounter with the natural surroundings at the destination. Ranger guides accompany tourists as they explore the national park and enjoy their nature walk experience.
Located: (Manyara – Tanzania), Lake Manyara National Park is 126 kilometers west of Arusha Town.
Activities: Birding, game drives, nature walks, canoeing, community visits

Arusha National Park
Arusha National Park is the closest national park to Arusha town – northern Tanzania’s safari capital. Arusha National Park is a gem of varied ecosystems, spectacular view of Mount Meru offering the opportunity to explore a beguiling diversity of habitats within few hours. The park is scenic and diverse which offers a wide range of animal and plant species.
The park is the only place in northern Tanzania where black and white colubus monkey can be easily spotted. In the middle of the forest is the Ngurdoto Crater containing many wetlands frequented by buffalos and warthogs. Giraffes, zebras and antelopes are frequently observed in the park. It is possible to see hyenas and leopards, lions and elephants are not common in Arusha National Park
Further north, you can admire the beauty of Momella lakes which have each a different color of blue and green. They support a rich variety of water birds and cobs defassa who love their wet shores.
One of the popular safari activities in Arusha National Park is a canoe or walking safari with an armed ranger. The Momela Lakes and Ngurdoto Crater can be visited in half a day at the beginning or at the end of a safari
Located: (Arusha - Tanzania), northeast of Arusha town. Arusha town is about 45 minutes away from Arusha National Park. Approximately 60 kilometers (35 miles) from Kilimanjaro International Airport
Activities: Forest walks, numerous picnic sites, game drive, canoeing
